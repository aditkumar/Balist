Eaten
  • Sissy's

    Great atmosphere and wonderful food. Get a bucket of chicken for the table.

  • The Palm

    Nice steakhouse, but best part is private rooms in the back. Make sure to ask for the "Southwest" room.

  • Perry's

    Amazing steakhouse. Very high-end atmosphere and food. Highly suggest the turtle soup and the pork chop.

  • The Velvet Taco

    Gourmet tacos (yes, those exist.) Pretty good, especially with their hotsauce. They also have some beers and a decent outdoor area.

  • Nonna

    Awesome Italian. Tough to get in so call a couple days in advance. Highly recommend their pastas and cocktails.

  • Bob's

    Nice steakhouse but lacking the atmosphere of Perry's or the privacy of The Palm back rooms. Still, absolutely awesome meat here.

  • Smoke

    Great high-end BBQ place. If it's on the menu get the Coffee Braised Brisket - it's a game-changer.

  • Meddlesome Moth

    Cool place, really big on small plates. A little chaotic with a big group - everyone is clamoring for stuff. Better with 2-6 people.

  • Deep Sushi

    Great little sushi spot in Deep Ellum (close to downtown). Not super high-end decor-wise but great service and tasty sushi. Patio is wonderful on a nice evening.

  • Serious Pizza

    Good place to go for a slice and a beer. In Deep Ellum so close to downtown. Have some TVs to watch games.

  • Oak

    Nice place. Very modern (in "design district"). Pricey, but food is fantastic.

  • Twisted Root Burger

    Pretty cool burger place in Deep Ellum. Very casual. Get the sweet potato chips.

  • Hopdoddy

    Awesome burger place. Delicious burgers and AMAZING beer selection. Some TVs around but definitely not a sports bar. Can't recommend enough.

  • Hibiscus

    Really nice high-end place. Grab a cocktail at the bar before sitting down - the bartenders are really knowledgable and will make you some samples if you're nice.

  • Canne Roso

    Gourmet pizzas. Food was great but not a lot of variety, so tough to order a bunch of interesting pizzas for the group. Best for groups of 2-4 I would say.

  • Chesterfield's

    Great little cocktail spot. They were undergoing a "transition" between chefs when I went though so the food was a bit underwhelming.
